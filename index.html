The History of Drag Racing

Drag racing is a motorsport involving two vehicles racing side-by-side on a straight track, typically a quarter mile long. It began in the 1940s and has since evolved into a highly competitive and technical sport.

  1. 1940s: Early grassroots races on empty streets and airstrips.
  2. 1950s: Organized drag racing started, led by the NHRA (National Hot Rod Association).
    • Hot rods became popular.
    • Tracks began to standardize.
  3. Present: Drag racing spans professional circuits and grassroots events.
